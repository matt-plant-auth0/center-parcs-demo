        Longleat Forest

        Exceeded Expectations

        @317andrewd- July 2021, Longleat Forest 

        "Having enjoyed overseas holidays for many years, we were faced with a staycation for 2021.

        Can’t say that I was bursting with excitement at the thought of staying in the U.K. with our unpredictable weather.

        I needn’t have worried. We booked a great lodge which was quite central (in the Pine area). Everything was clean and tidy and everything worked. Despite a regular turnover of visitors, the lodge felt quite new. The beds are firm, so you may want to take your own pillows. The lodge had a great layout which encouraged family gatherings for our group of 6 which included mum, dad, 2 daughters, 2 partners and granddaughter. This was the first time that we’d all been away in a family group and the relaxed atmosphere of the park helped.

        The friendliness and willingness of all the staff in all areas really was noticeable and stood out; this played a large part in us enjoying our stay. 10/10 to the staff.

        The site was clean and tidy and holidaymakers’ cars were parked safely away on the main car park making for safe and free movement around the park. Fellow holidaymakers were also friendly and willing to share a smile or a conversation.

        The activities were all enjoyable (Segway, falconry, tree top walk, massages, archery and pool) although the race to book them in advance when you were able to was a little tiring, with options disappearing quickly before your very eyes. When booking activities, ensure that you leave enough time to transit between them as the site is big, especially if some of your family members aren’t that mobile.

        A bike, either hired or personal, is a must to get around as the site is big. Electric assisted bikes would help with the hills as the park is quite hilly. The land train was regularly on time and ran every 25 mins. Factor this into your scheduling of activities if needed but beware that it can fill up quickly at peak times.

        The scenery is fantastic with tall pine trees and an impressive lake making you feel that you were in a distant forest away from the U.K.

        Longleat Safari Park is only a few miles away and well worth a visit. I know the two businesses are separate from each other but I’m surprised that some kind of discount isn’t available as it would draw more crowds to the safari park. We visited on the day we left as checkout was at 10am and it seemed a logical way to spend the final day.

        Despite my reservations of a U.K. staycation, we’re thinking about rebooking for next year, which, for me, is practically unheard of!

        I was very pleasantly surprised and would recommend it."

        Great holiday with 4 teenagers!!

        @MRMT8- July 2021, Woburn Forest

        "This was our first experience of Center Parcs and I was a little unsure of what to expect. It is quite a different holiday to what we are used to - usually we are sun & pool in a nice hotel! I have to say we were very pleasantly surprised. The lodges are all modern and clean. Kitchen is well equipped (fridge, dishwasher, oven, MW). Bathrooms are modern and well designed with heated towel rails. Bedrooms are neat with each person having their own single wardrobe. No washing/laundry facilities fyi. There is a large Tesco not too far away.

        In terms of choosing a lodge - you don't have to btw, I hadn't understood that you can drive to your lodge to load and unload, so we picked a lodge near the car park. It actually ended up suiting us quite well as it was away from the activity areas and sort of in the middle of the 3 locations you go to - Plaza/waterfront, Village Square and Outdoor Activity Centre. So it worked out really well for us. However, it did mean downhill to the Plaza and Village Square (so uphill on the return) which could be an issue for some but gave us all a bit more exercise!

        We pre-booked most of our activities on a friend's advice and that I would say is a must. Bookings open about 2 weeks before you leave and I booked everything on the first day it opened. I recommend doing your research beforehand in terms of what activities are available and what you'd actually like to do and make a rough plan as that will make the booking process so much easier! It's not the easiest to find how much they cost until you're booking.

        Due to covid restrictions we couldn't use the pool facilities the whole time but had 4 X 2 hour slots for our week and to be honest that was perfect for us. The huge huge plus was that this meant the pool was never crowded and there were no queues for the slides or water rapids etc. This made for a really pleasant and enjoyable time at the pool.

        The grounds were all really well kept, as were all the activities. All the activities were great. The kids (age 14 & 16) loved them all. If you can afford it, I'd book as many as you can. I booked so they had about 3 activities in the day to keep them busy and then they'd go off for a ride on the bikes - wanted to keep them off their phones! Woburn is not that spread out so it is possible to walk everywhere but the kids had great fun being able to go out on the bikes with no cars around. It is not on the flat so I ended up pushing my bike up the hill a few times but it was fine.

        The lake is a little on the small side but they have made the most of what there is with what's on offer. Half an hour on a kayak or pedalo is more than enough given its small size. Recommend breakfast at the Pancake House by the lake - lovely sitting outside on a sunny morning!

        The archery activities went down really well with our 4 teenagers so would highly recommend them. As the description mentions, you do get taught what to do but they keep it nice a simple so you are really participating for most of your time slot which is great.
        Aerial adventure (make sure you book the age appropriate option) was challenging enough for them and has a great zipwire across the lake. They also enjoyed the Drop, Escape the Forest, Segway, Laser Combat, Laser Clay Shoot. Geocache was nice as it gave you a walk through the place but wasn't challenging, so some older kids may find that a little slow. We also booked badminton, tennis, squash, bowling, mini golf, football pool and they enjoyed them all. Some of these cheaper options I added into the itinerary when we were there. I wish we had tried the fencing.

        The staff at the activities were all brilliant - really invested in making sure you have a good time and that made it so much more enjoyable - really well done Center Parcs!

        I'd highly recommend this as an active holiday for the family. We had 4 teenagers with us and they are all still raving about the holiday and all the stuff they got to do and to be honest the covid restrictions of allocated pool slots made for an even better experience."

        Woburn Forest Tripadvisor
